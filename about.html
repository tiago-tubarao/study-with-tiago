About Tiago

Nursing student. ADHD brain. Building study tools that actually work.

Why I Make These

I'm a BSN nursing student with ADHD. Traditional study methods — reading the textbook, highlighting notes, making flashcards — don't work for my brain. I need to talk through it, see it visually, and test myself constantly to make anything stick.

So I started making YouTube videos where I break down pharmacology drugs in plain language. Not reading from the textbook — actually explaining it like I'm teaching a friend.

Then I thought: what if I could organize what I'm teaching against what nursing programs actually cover?

That's what this site does. Every drug I cover in a video gets compared against standard pharmacology topics. You can see exactly what I covered, what's typically tested, and what you might want to focus on. Then you test yourself with NCLEX-style scenario questions I wrote from scratch.

This isn't a study tool I downloaded. I built it. Because I needed it to exist, and it didn't.

My Study Philosophy

1. If I can't explain it simply, I don't know it.
Every video forces me to break a drug down to its core. If I'm stumbling, I go back and study more.

2. Organized against the curriculum.
I don't just make slides and hope they're right. I compare every drug against standard pharmacology curricula and the NCLEX test plan. If it's not in scope, it's flagged. If it's missing from my video, it's marked.

3. Test yourself, then learn.
The confidence dots on this site don't just rate how you feel — they open up scenario questions that test if you ACTUALLY know it. Get the questions first. Explanations after.

4. ADHD-friendly everything.
Short sections. Color coding. Interactive elements. Timestamps to jump around. No walls of text. If it doesn't hold my attention, it won't hold yours.
