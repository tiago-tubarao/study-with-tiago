Drug Card Checklist

A printable checklist for building nursing drug cards without missing the parts that usually show up in questions.

Educational study tool only. Always verify medications, doses, contraindications, and monitoring with your current textbook, instructor, facility policy, and drug guide.

For every drug, check these boxes

  • Class: What drug family is it in?
  • Prototype / look-alikes: What other drugs sound or act like this?
  • Why it is given: What problem is it treating?
  • Expected effect: What should improve if it works?
  • Big adverse effects: What can go wrong that matters for nursing care?
  • Red flags: What finding means stop, hold, call, or assess immediately?
  • Labs / vitals: What needs to be checked before or during therapy?
  • Contraindications / cautions: Who should not get it, or who needs closer monitoring?
  • Interactions: What cannot mix safely?
  • Antidote / reversal: Is there a reversal agent or emergency treatment?
  • Patient teaching: What does the patient need to know at home?
  • Exam trap: What answer choice would look tempting but be unsafe?

Quick compare box

When two drugs blur together, write one sentence for each:

  • This drug is mainly for...
  • The other drug is mainly for...
  • The easiest way I tell them apart is...
