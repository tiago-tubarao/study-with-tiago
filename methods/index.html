🛡️ Method 1 — The Verbal Underline Protocol

The problem: ATI proctored doesn't let you highlight, cross-out, or annotate. So when a question says "which response indicates the client needs MORE teaching?" — your brain auto-fills the verb you EXPECT (correct teaching) and you pick the wrong answer.

I missed this trap THREE times in 24 hours before my Med-Surg Proctored. Same trap. Knew the rule. Discipline failed.

The fix: an internal version of underlining. 4 mental steps before clicking ANY answer.

The 4-Step Routine

  1. READ THE VERB OUT LOUD (in your head) — emphasize it: "INCORRECT teaching" · "NEEDS MORE teaching" · "PRIORITY action"
  2. READ THE MODIFIER — "4 hours" · "first" · "immediately" · "after teaching" · "24 hours"
  3. DECODE ANY PREFIX — AB- · AD- · HYPO- · HYPER- · BRADY- · TACHY- · HEMI- · TETRA-
  4. FLIP CHECK — Ask yourself: "Am I being asked for the RIGHT answer or the WRONG answer?" — then answer.

Why it works: forces your prefrontal cortex (slow/deliberate) to override basal ganglia pattern-matching (fast/automatic). Per Barkley's executive function framework — externalize the discipline.

Memory hook: "If your brain says 'I knew that' AFTER picking — you skipped the verbal underline."

🧠 Method 2 — Cognitive Collision Traps

The problem: ADHD brain pattern-matches phonetically-similar words to the WRONG concept. Or it second-guesses a clean answer into a reverse-pick. Two collision patterns I named myself.

Pattern A — "I Outsmarted Myself"

Saw "void every 4 hours" as a UTI teaching answer. My brain said: "4 hours = holding it = BAD." I REVERSE-picked AWAY from the right answer.

The actual logic: "every 4 hours" is a FLOOR not a ceiling — pee MORE if you need to, just don't go LONGER than 4 hours. Stasis = bacteria multiply = UTI.

Defense: Don't outsmart a clean answer. If it LOOKS right on first read, trust it unless a specific qualifier flips it.

Pattern B — Phonetic Prefix Collision

Stem said abduction pillow post-hip arthroplasty. My brain went: "abduction → abs → abdomen → splinting for cough." Picked logic about coughing. Wrong.

Correct: abduction pillow keeps legs APART to prevent ADduction = prevents the new hip from dislocating.

The Lock

Hip Dislocation Triad — The New Hip Pops Out If…

  1. ADduction (legs cross midline)
  2. Flexion (hip bent past 90°)
  3. Internal rotation (toes turn inward)

→ Abduction pillow blocks #1. Don't cross legs. Don't bend past 90°. Don't twist toes inward.

Other Phonetic Collision Risks

  • HYPO vs HYPER — one letter, opposite directions
  • BRADY vs TACHY — often share modifier patterns
  • AMLODIPINE vs AMILORIDE — look-alike drug names
  • HYDRAlazine vs HYDROXYzine — sound-alike
  • LABETALOL vs LAMOTRIGINE — sound-alike
  • CELEBREX vs CEREBYX — sound-alike

Memory hook: "AB = AWAY. AD = ADD-toward."
